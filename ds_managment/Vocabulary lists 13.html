About unit

This module contains two vocabulary sections:

  • Core Terminology & Concepts (V.1) — обязательный минимум. Это основа для понимания темы.
  • Functional Language: Essential Phrases & Questions (V.3) — готовые фразы для реальной жизни. Старайтесь применять их на занятиях и в рабочих разговорах, чтобы звучать естественно.

Core Terminology & Concepts

Term Part of Speech Translation English Definition
Tech stack Noun Phrase Технологический стек The set of technologies, frameworks, and tools used to build a project.
Mentor / Mentee Noun Наставник / Подопечный An experienced advisor (Mentor) and the person they guide (Mentee) in a developmental relationship.
Peer / Colleague Noun Коллега, равный по статусу A professional of similar seniority or role, with whom you can exchange knowledge and support in a non-hierarchical way.
Alumni Network Noun Phrase Сообщество выпускников A professional network of individuals who graduated from the same university or program, often a valuable source of connections.
Industry Event / Meetup Noun Phrase Отраслевое мероприятие / митап A conference, workshop, or informal gathering where professionals in a specific field connect and share knowledge.
Virtual Coffee / Chat Noun Phrase Виртуальный кофе / чат An informal, short video call (15-30 mins) used for networking or catching up, replacing an in-person coffee meeting.
Talking Points / Bullet Points Noun Phrase Тезисы для выступления / пункты Key ideas or achievements prepared in advance to guide a conversation, ensuring you communicate your main messages clearly.
Cold Outreach / Backchannel Noun Phrase Холодный контакт / Неформальный канал связи Contacting someone professionally without prior connection or introduction. Requires a highly personalized and respectful approach.
Elevator Pitch Noun Phrase Краткая самопрезентация (лифтовая презентация) A concise, compelling summary of who you are, what you do, and what value you bring—deliverable in 30-60 seconds.
Gatekeeper / Decision Maker Noun Привратник / Лицо, принимающее решение A person who controls access to someone else (Gatekeeper) or has the authority to make hiring/business decisions (Decision Maker).
Self-Presentation & Career Management
To specialize in... Verb Phrase Специализироваться на... A professional way to define your main area of expertise in ML/AI.
To be focused on... Verb Phrase Быть сфокусированным на... Describes your current project or the problem you are solving right now.
To put yourself out there Phrasal Verb Проявить себя, заявить о себе To step outside your comfort zone by sharing your ideas, presenting your work, or applying for visible opportunities.
To advocate for yourself Verb Phrase Отстаивать свои интересы To confidently and professionally communicate your achievements, aspirations, and needs (e.g., for a promotion, project, or resources).
To map out your career goals Phrasal Verb Спланировать свои карьерные цели To consciously define and plan the steps needed to achieve your desired mid- to long-term career progression.
To negotiate an offer Verb Phrase Вести переговоры по оферу To discuss and agree on the terms of a job offer (salary, benefits, etc.).
To send a thank-you note Verb Phrase Отправить благодарственное письмо To email interviewers after a meeting to express gratitude and reiterate interest.
To debrief after an interview Verb Phrase Провести разбор после собеседования To analyze your own performance and note areas for improvement.
Project Ownership & Impact
To take ownership (of a project / metric) Verb Phrase Взять ответственность (за проект / метрику) To voluntarily assume end-to-end accountability for success, demonstrating leadership beyond assigned tasks.
To drive a project to completion Verb Phrase Довести проект до завершения To proactively manage timelines, stakeholders, and obstacles to ensure delivery of tangible value.
To quantify your impact Verb Phrase Количественно оценить свой вклад To measure and communicate results in concrete metrics (e.g., "improved accuracy by 15%", "reduced processing time by 2 hours").
To translate technical findings into business value Verb Phrase Перевести технические находки в бизнес-ценность To reframe complex results into clear statements about revenue, cost, risk, or efficiency for non-technical decision-makers.
To build a business case Verb Phrase Обосновать бизнес-целесообразность To create a data-driven argument (ROI, opportunity cost) to secure resources or approval for a new initiative.
To showcase your work Verb Phrase Продемонстрировать свою работу To strategically present projects and results to the right audiences (e.g., in demo sessions, newsletters) to increase visibility.
To document your process Verb Phrase Документировать свой процесс To create clear records (notebooks, wikis) of methodology and decisions, enabling knowledge sharing, reproducibility, and onboarding.
To whiteboard a solution Verb Phrase Рисовать решение на доске To sketch out an architecture or algorithm visually during an interview.
Communication, Leadership & Collaboration
To break the ice Idiom Начать беседу To say or do something that makes people feel more relaxed in a social setting.
To reach out / To reach out to someone Phrasal Verb Связаться / Написать кому-либо To initiate contact with someone professionally (e.g., for advice, collaboration, or informational interview).
To follow up (after a meeting / conversation) Phrasal Verb Написать / связаться (после встречи / разговора) To send a brief, polite message to reaffirm a point, share promised information, or simply thank someone, solidifying the connection.
To speak up in meetings Verb Phrase Высказываться на совещаниях To contribute valuable insights, ask clarifying questions, and shape discussions, establishing yourself as an engaged thought partner.
To mentor a junior colleague Verb Phrase Быть ментором для младшего коллеги To guide, advise, and share knowledge with a less experienced teammate, contributing to team growth and demonstrating leadership.
To step up (and lead / take on more) Phrasal Verb Взять на себя больше, проявить инициативу To voluntarily fill a leadership vacuum or take on stretch assignments during critical moments, demonstrating initiative.
To brainstorm (ideas / solutions) together Verb Мозговой штурм (идей / решений) совместно To collaboratively generate ideas, often in a system design or pairing interview.
To ask clarifying questions Verb Phrase Задавать уточняющие вопросы To seek more details to ensure you fully understand a problem or question.
Professional Growth & Learning
To stay up-to-date with the field Verb Phrase Быть в курсе последних тенденций в области To continuously learn about new tools, techniques, and research in data science through reading, courses, and conferences.
To brush up on (a skill / topic) Phrasal Verb Освежить в памяти (навык / тему) To proactively review and practice a skill or area of knowledge you haven't used recently to regain proficiency.
To seek out feedback Verb Phrase Активно искать обратную связь To proactively ask for constructive criticism on your work or performance from peers, managers, or stakeholders to identify areas for growth.
To act on feedback Verb Phrase Действовать по полученной обратной связи To demonstrably incorporate received feedback, showing adaptability, humility, and commitment to improvement.
To grow your professional network Verb Phrase Расширять профессиональную сеть контактов To systematically increase the number and quality of your professional connections.
To network (with peers / in the community) Verb Нетворкинг (с коллегами / в комьюнити) To intentionally build and maintain professional relationships by attending events, connecting on LinkedIn, and having conversations.

Functional Language: Essential Phrases & Questions

Phrase / Construction Use Case Definition &Translation
Technical Interview Communication
The core challenge wasn't the model itself, but the data pipeline. — Looking back, the core challenge wasn't the model itself, but the data pipeline and its reliability. To shift focus from algorithmic hype to real-world engineering hurdles, demonstrating practical experience.

— Основной сложностью была не модель, а пайплайн данных и его надежность.

We faced a trade-off between X and Y. We chose X because... — We faced a trade-off between accuracy and latency. We chose a simpler model because it aligned with our core business constraint, which was real-time response. To show mature decision-making by explicitly naming trade-offs and linking them to business objectives.

— Мы столкнулись с компромиссом между точностью и задержкой. Мы выбрали более простую модель, потому что это соответствовало нашему ключевому бизнес-ограничению — времени отклика в реальном времени.

My first step would be to isolate the issue... — If the model degrades in production, my first step would be to isolate the issue: check for data drift, then validate feature consistency, and only then examine the model. To demonstrate a systematic, calm, and logical approach to debugging production systems.

— Если производительность модели в продакшене падает, моим первым шагом была бы изоляция проблемы: проверка на дрейф данных, затем согласованность фичей, и только потом — анализ модели.

I'd frame that as a hypothesis, not a conclusion. — That's an interesting observation. I'd frame that as a hypothesis, not a conclusion. We could validate it quickly by running an A/B test on the new user cohort. To maintain scientific rigor in discussions and steer conversations toward actionable, data-driven validation.

— Это интересное наблюдение. Я бы сформулировал это как гипотезу, а не вывод. Мы могли бы быстро проверить ее, запустив A/B-тест на когорте новых пользователей.

The metric improved by X%, but more importantly... — The metric improved by 15%, but more importantly, it reduced the variance in outcomes, which was the real business pain point we were trying to solve. To show you understand that not all metric improvements are equal and can connect technical results to core business value.

— Метрика улучшилась на 15%, но что важнее — снизилась дисперсия результатов, что и было настоящей бизнес-проблемой, которую мы пытались решить.

I'm not familiar with that specific library, but based on the problem description... — I'm not familiar with that specific library, but based on the problem description, I would approach it similarly to how I've used Scikit-learn for building ensemble classifiers. To demonstrate problem-solving skills and the ability to transfer knowledge, rather than just reciting memorized tools.

— Я не знаком с этой конкретной библиотекой, но, судя по описанию задачи, я подошел бы к ней так же, как использовал Scikit-learn для построения ансамблевых классификаторов.

Our confidence intervals were wide at first. To tighten them, we focused on... — Our confidence intervals were wide at first. To tighten them, we focused on collecting more data in the underrepresented segments rather than just tuning the model hyperparameters. To show strategic thinking about the root cause of statistical uncertainty (data quality/quantity) rather than just algorithmic tweaking.

— Наши доверительные интервалы сначала были широкими. Чтобы сузить их, мы сосредоточились на сборе большего количества данных в непредставленных сегментах, а не просто на тонкой настройке гиперпараметров модели.

"It depends" is my starting point. The right solution depends on... — Which model is best? "It depends" is my starting point. The right solution depends on the latency requirements, the update frequency of the data, and the cost of an error for the application. To avoid dogmatic answers and immediately frame the response within critical real-world constraints.

— Какая модель лучше? «Это зависит» — моя отправная точка. Правильное решение зависит от требований к задержке, частоты обновления данных и стоимости ошибки для данного приложения.

Networking & Research Dialogue
I’ve been following your work on… It's been a game-changer. — I've been following your work on detecting silent model failures in production. Your concept of using prediction entropy as a leading indicator for data drift has been a game-changer for our team. We're now piloting it as an early warning system in in our deployment pipeline. To initiate a research dialogue by demonstrating sustained engagement with a colleague's work, citing a specific, actionable idea, and emphasizing its high impact on your own practice.

— Я слежу за вашими работами по обнаружению скрытых сбоев моделей в продакшене. Ваша концепция использования энтропии предсказаний как опережающего индикатора дрейфа данных стала для нашей команды настоящим прорывом. Сейчас мы пилотируем этот подход как систему раннего предупреждения в нашем пайплайне внедрения.

Your work on [Specific Topic] really resonated with me... — Your work on few-shot learning really resonated with me, especially the part about prompt engineering. It made me reconsider my approach to our text classification project. To initiate a meaningful, flattering, and specific dialogue that shows you've done your homework and are genuinely engaged.

— Ваша работа по few-shot learning нашла у меня большой отклик, особенно часть про инженерию промптов. Это заставило меня пересмотреть мой подход к нашему проекту по классификации текстов.

That's a fascinating result. I'm curious, was the decision to use [Method A] over [Method B] driven more by...? — That's a fascinating result. I'm curious, was the decision to use a Transformer over an LSTM driven more by theoretical elegance or computational constraints in your setup? To ask a sophisticated, open-ended question that sparks a deeper discussion about design choices, beyond the surface-level presentation.

— Это захватывающий результат. Мне интересно, выбор Transformer вместо LSTM был продиктован больше теоретической элегантностью или вычислительными ограничениями в вашей системе?

In our experiments, we also observed [X]. However, we found that... — In our experiments, we also observed a performance plateau. However, we found that increasing the batch size significantly altered the outcome. What was your experience with that? To contribute to a discussion as a peer, sharing your findings while showing respect and curiosity about the other person's work.

— В наших экспериментах мы тоже наблюдали плато производительности. Однако мы обнаружили, что увеличение размера батча существенно меняло результат. Какой у вас был опыт с этим?

I'm currently exploring [Your Research Area] with a focus on [Your Angle]... — I'm currently exploring time-series forecasting with a focus on anomaly detection. I'd be very grateful for a quick take on the biggest practical hurdle you foresee in productionizing such models. To ask for valuable, specific advice in a respectful and concise manner, making it easy for a busy person to respond.

— В настоящее время я изучаю прогнозирование временных рядов с фокусом на обнаружение аномалий. Я был бы очень признателен за краткое мнение о самом большом практическом препятствии, которое вы предвидите при выводе таких моделей в продакшен.

That's a bold approach. I'm wondering about the scalability to... — That's a bold approach. I'm wondering about the scalability to much larger datasets. Have you done any stress-testing on that front with, say, 10 million samples? To ask a constructive, forward-looking question that helps the speaker (and audience) think about practical limitations and future work, without being dismissive.

— Это смелый подход. Мне интересно насчет масштабируемости на значительно больших датасетах. Вы проводили стресс-тестирование в этом направлении, скажем, на 10 миллионах образцов?

We hit a similar wall with [problem]. Our workaround was... — We hit a similar wall with vanishing gradients in our RNN. Our workaround was gradient clipping, but it felt like a hack. I'd love to compare notes on more elegant solutions. To create instant rapport by showing vulnerability (your solution wasn't perfect) and inviting collaboration, turning a monologue into a dialogue.

— Мы столкнулись с похожей проблемой затухающих градиентов в нашей RNN. Нашим решением было gradient clipping, но это казалось костылем. Я бы с удовольствием обменялся мнениями о более элегантных решениях.

Beyond the X, what was the most surprising side-effect...? — Beyond the accuracy gains, what was the most surprising side-effect or insight you observed during the deployment of this model? To ask a unique, reflective question that often reveals more interesting stories and personal experiences than standard technical queries.

— Помимо роста точности, каким был самый неожиданный побочный эффект или инсайт, который вы заметили во время развертывания этой модели?

Let's continue this conversation. Are you on [messenger_name]? — Let's continue this conversation. Are you on Telegram? I can share the preprint I mentioned and we can discuss it there. To naturally transition a live conversation to an ongoing digital connection via a preferred platform for tech communities, with a clear purpose (sharing materials).

— Давайте продолжим этот разговор. Вы есть в…? Я могу скинуть упомянутый препринт, и мы сможем обсудить его там.

I found your insights incredibly practical. I run a small study group... — I found your insights incredibly practical. I run a small study group on MLOps on Telegram. Would you be interested in joining as a guest for one session? To offer a platform and audience for the person, showing you value their expertise and are connected to an active learning community. This is a high-value invitation.

— Ваши инсайты показались мне невероятно практичными. Я веду небольшую study group по MLOps в Telegram. Вас бы заинтересовало присоединиться в качестве гостя на одну сессию?
